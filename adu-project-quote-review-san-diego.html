Got an ADU Quote? Send It Before You Sign.

ADU builds in San Diego are high-value, high-complexity projects where the difference between a fair bid and an inflated one can be $50,000 or more. We'll review your quote for free. No design-build affiliations, no referral fees. Just honest eyes on the numbers before you commit.

📋 What a Solid ADU Quote Should Include

  • ADU type clearly specified — detached, attached, garage conversion (JADU), or manufactured
  • Square footage and number of bedrooms/bathrooms spelled out, matching permitted plans
  • Contractor's California CSLB license number and class (Class B General or specific trades)
  • Architectural/engineering plans — included in bid or explicitly excluded with cost estimate
  • Permit fees itemized — City of San Diego ADU permits vary widely by scope
  • Site prep: grading, drainage, and utility connections (water, sewer, electrical) detailed
  • Foundation type specified (slab, pier, stem wall) with soil condition notes if applicable
  • Framing, insulation, drywall, roofing all included or explicitly excluded
  • Kitchen and bathroom fixtures specified by brand/quality tier
  • Electrical panel upgrade scope — many ADU projects require upgrading the main panel
  • HVAC/mini-split system specified
  • Timeline: permitting phase, construction start, and estimated completion
  • Payment schedule tied to construction milestones, not calendar dates
  • Warranty on labor (1 year minimum in California) and subcontractor list

⚠️ Red Flags That Should Make You Pause

  • Quote does not include permit costs or says "permits not needed" — ADU builds always require permits in San Diego
  • No architectural/design drawings included or accounted for — this is a major cost often hidden in "turnkey" pricing
  • Deposit required over 10% before permits are pulled (California deposit law applies)
  • Utility connection scope ("tie-in") missing — hooking up water and sewer can add $10,000–$30,000
  • Vague scope like "build 400 sq ft ADU as discussed" with no specs
  • Bid excludes appliances, fixtures, or finish materials — ask specifically what's included
  • No mention of panel upgrade assessment — an unplanned panel upgrade mid-project is a common cost overrun
  • Timeline not specified — open-ended timelines typically favor the contractor
  • No CSLB license number or license cannot be verified
  • Pressure to sign quickly without time to review or compare bids

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ADU quote review really free?

Yes. Quote review is always free at SideGuy. We don't take referral fees from ADU builders and we don't sell design or build services. Our only interest is giving you honest guidance before you sign a six-figure contract.

How much does an ADU cost to build in San Diego in 2026?

A detached ADU in San Diego typically runs $275–$425 per square foot all-in (including permits, design, and site work). A 500 sq ft unit: $140,000–$215,000. A 1,200 sq ft unit: $330,000–$510,000. Garage conversions run $80,000–$150,000 depending on existing structure. Manufactured/prefab ADUs can be less but have their own site-work cost considerations.

How long does it take to build an ADU in San Diego?

From initial planning to certificate of occupancy, expect 12–18 months for a detached ADU. Permitting alone typically takes 3–6 months through the City of San Diego, though pre-approved ADU plans can shorten that. Garage conversions run faster — 6–10 months total is realistic.

Do I need a separate meter for my ADU?

Not always required, but often advisable if you plan to rent the unit. SDG&E and the city have rules about separate metering. Your contractor should be able to explain the options and the cost difference. If they can't, that's a gap in their ADU expertise.

What if I'm using an ADU design-build company — do they still need review?

Especially then. Design-build ADU companies in San Diego have grown rapidly in response to state law changes, and quality varies significantly. Some are excellent; others are undercapitalized or have thin margins that lead to value engineering mid-project. The same review criteria apply.

Can I finance an ADU build in San Diego?

Yes — several options: HELOC, cash-out refinance, construction loan, or CalHFA ADU Grant Program (up to $40,000 for eligible homeowners). Financing terms significantly affect the real total cost of your ADU. If the builder is quoting you a "monthly payment" without discussing loan terms, text us before you proceed.

What if the contractor pushes back on me wanting a review?

That's a significant flag. A legitimate ADU builder — especially one asking for $150,000+ — has no reason to object to a homeowner taking 24 hours to have an independent party review the proposal. Resistance to scrutiny on a major contract is itself a reason to reconsider.
