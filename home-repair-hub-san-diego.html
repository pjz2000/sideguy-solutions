❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a problem is urgent or can wait?

Text us what you're seeing. Water intrusion, electrical burning smells, and foundation movement are usually urgent. Cosmetic cracks and minor leaks often aren't — but we'll tell you which is which.

How much do home repairs cost in San Diego?

San Diego is one of the most expensive repair markets in the US. HVAC tune-ups run $80–$200. Roof repairs $300–$2,000+. Plumbing leaks $150–$600 for easy jobs. We provide current San Diego-specific ranges so you're not surprised.

Can you review a contractor quote before I sign?

Yes. This is one of the most valuable things we do. Send us the quote — we'll flag anything unusual, tell you if the price is in range, and point out missing items that should be in any good contract.

My landlord is ignoring a repair. What do I do?

San Diego has tenant-protection rules. Depending on the issue (habitability, mold, broken heat/AC), you may have formal rights. Text us what's happening — we can walk you through the escalation process.

Do I need a permit for my repair or renovation?

Many San Diego repairs require permits — electrical panel work, structural changes, new HVAC systems, ADUs. Unpermitted work causes problems when you sell. We'll help you understand when you need one.

What are red flags when hiring a San Diego contractor?

Demands for large cash deposits upfront, no CSLB license, no written contract, pressure to decide today, and vague scope of work. We'll review any contractor before you commit.

Can you help me find a trustworthy contractor?

Yes. We can help you vet contractors, compare quotes, and understand what to look for. Text us your project — we'll give you a framework for selecting the right person.
