Who to call for a leak under the sink in San Diego?

SideGuy: Clarity before selling — this page documents what changed, why it's confusing, and neutral options; it is informational, not a sales pitch.

A leak under the sink is one of those problems that looks small but can become expensive fast if left alone. This page helps you understand whether this is a quick DIY fix, a handyman job, or a plumber’s call—and what to do right now to limit damage.

Quick triage (do this first)

  • Shut off the cabinet supply valves or the main water if needed to stop running water.
  • Clear items from the cabinet and put a bucket or towels under the leak to catch water.
  • Try to identify the source: supply line, drain trap (P-trap), or faucet connection.

Who to call

  • Plumber: If the leak is from a pipe, drain trap, or you see water running constantly, call a plumber.
  • Handyman: For minor leaks at flexible supply lines, loose connections, or replacing a faucet, a qualified handyman may suffice.
  • Emergency service: If water is flooding, electrics are wet, or you can’t stop the flow—treat it as an emergency and call a 24/7 plumber.

Estimated cost in San Diego: simple repairs (tighten or replace a fitting) often run $75–$250. If pipes need replacement or access is difficult, costs rise. Get a quick photo, ask for a diagnosis cost, and prefer transparent pricing.

Local tips

  • Ask for a local license and proof of insurance.
  • Take photos and ask for a short explanation of the fix before work starts.
  • If the problem looks like mold or long-term leakage, document for insurance purposes.
