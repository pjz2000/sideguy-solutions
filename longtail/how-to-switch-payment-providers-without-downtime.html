How to switch payment providers without downtime

SideGuy: Clarity before selling — this page documents what changed, why it's confusing, and neutral options; it is informational, not a sales pitch.

What changed (facts only)

Payment platforms and processors now offer more self-service tools and integration options, but migration procedures vary and require coordination between technical and finance teams.

Why this confused people

Different providers use varying terminology, have different test modes and webhook behaviors, and statements may be hard to compare directly—making migrations risky without a checklist.

What breaks if you ignore it

  • Customer payments may fail or be double-charged.
  • Recurring subscriptions can be disrupted, leading to revenue loss and customer support overhead.
  • Refunds and disputes may be mishandled if webhooks or reconciliation are not tested.

What options exist now (neutral)

  • Run a parallel integration: keep old provider active while testing the new one.
  • Use a test customer group and staged switchover times (low-traffic windows).
  • Ensure clear reconciliation and rollback processes before flipping live traffic.
