Who to Call for an Under-Sink Leak in Encinitas

Clarity before selling: This page explains your options; we do not recommend vendors—call for estimates from licensed local pros.

Quick overview

Under-sink leaks can be caused by loose fittings, damaged P-traps, or worn valves. If you can safely turn off the water and apply a temporary fix, do so; otherwise call a licensed plumber.

Who to call

  • Licensed plumber (general plumbing license)
  • Handyman for minor non-pressurized drips (only if small and safe)
  • Emergency plumber for major flooding or if shutoff is not working

What to expect

Initial visit: 30–60 minutes. Typical fixes: tighten fittings, replace P-trap, or replace a valve. Cost ballpark: $120–$450 depending on parts and time.

Pre-visit checklist

  1. Turn off the local shutoff valve under sink (if available)
  2. Clear the cabinet so tech can work
  3. Take photos of the leak area

Next steps

Get 2–3 quotes, confirm license and insurance, ask about warranties on parts and labor, and request photos of the repair if possible.

FAQ

Can I use tape or epoxy for a temporary fix?
Temporary measures can stem minor drips but replace permanent parts ASAP; some fixes are not safe for pressurized lines.
How long does a typical repair take?
Most under-sink repairs take 30–90 minutes depending on complexity.
