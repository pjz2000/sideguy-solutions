AI ethics for small businesses — simple rules to follow

SideGuy: Clarity before selling — this page explains what changed, why it confuses people, what can break if ignored, and neutral options.

What changed (facts only)

  • Increased use of AI in customer-facing roles and for administrative decisions.

Why this confused people

Unclear consent flows and varying vendor promises about fairness and bias mitigation.

What breaks if you ignore it

Customer complaints, biased outcomes, and regulatory risk.

What options exist now (neutral)

Audit training data, use human-in-loop for high-risk decisions, and keep simple logs for accountability.

If this affected you, text PJ

Text PJ

  • Get consent for customer data when needed.
  • Keep humans in the decision loop for higher-risk actions.
  • Log decisions and keep a small audit trail for important workflows.
