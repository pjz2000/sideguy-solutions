# SideGuy — When Not to Hire Help Library  
**Public Trust & Anti-Regret System**

---

## Core Purpose
- Reduce regret  
- Prevent unnecessary spending  
- Normalize doing nothing  
- Build extreme trust by saying NO publicly  

This library exists because avoiding a bad hire is a win.

---

## Positioning (Locked)
- “Sometimes the smartest move is not hiring anyone”
- “Clarity before cost”
- “Avoiding a bad hire is success”

---

## What This Library Is
- A calm, honest reference
- Pages that explain when help is unnecessary
- Written for people who feel unsure, rushed, or pressured

---

## What This Library Is NOT
- Not anti-help  
- Not shaming  
- Not dismissive  
- Not sarcastic  

---

## Canonical Page Titles (Use Exactly)
- When hiring help is a waste of money  
- Do I actually need help for this?  
- What happens if I wait a week?  
- When doing nothing is the right call  
- Why people overhire help  
- How to avoid regret after hiring help  

---

## Required Page Structure
1. Acknowledge the feeling (stress, guilt, urgency)  
2. Explain why people feel pressured to hire  
3. List common scenarios where help is unnecessary  
4. Explain risks of hiring too fast  
5. Give explicit permission to pause  
6. Offer a calm clarity conversation (Text PJ)  

---

## Language Rules
Use phrases like:
- “You’re not wrong for hesitating”
- “This can usually wait”
- “Most people don’t need help here”
- “It’s okay to sit with this”

Never use judgmental language.

---

## Examples to Include
- Small tasks that feel bigger than they are  
- One-off problems that resolve themselves  
- Emotional decisions made under stress  
- Situations where friends, routines, or time are enough  

---

## Anti-Sales Safeguards
- If the page nudges hiring → rewrite it  
- No upsells  
- No pricing language  
- No CTAs except calm conversation  

---

## Internal Linking Rules

- Build a thinking library, not funnels

## Final Rule
If unsure, err on the side of restraint.

**The NO is the product here.**
