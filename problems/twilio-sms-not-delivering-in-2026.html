Twilio SMS Not Delivering in 2026: 6 Fixes That Actually Work

Your Twilio messages are sending without errors but never arriving. Or they're showing "delivered" in the console but the recipient never got them. Both symptoms are maddening because the API returns 200 and you have no immediate clue where the message died. This guide walks through the real causes — in order of likelihood — and tells you exactly what to fix.

A2P 10DLC: The #1 Cause of SMS Failures in 2026

If you're sending business SMS in the US without A2P 10DLC registration, carriers are silently filtering your messages — no error, no bounce, just missing delivery. This changed in 2023 and is now strictly enforced.

What you need:

  • Brand registration (your business entity) in Twilio Console
  • Campaign registration (use case: marketing, 2FA, notifications, etc.)
  • Number assignment to your registered campaign
  • Sample messages that match your actual message templates

Registration takes 2–5 business days. Until approved, your messages may deliver inconsistently or not at all to T-Mobile, AT&T, and Verizon subscribers.

Most likely causes

  • A2P 10DLC not registered — the single largest cause in 2026
  • Message content triggers carrier spam filter (URLs, promo language, unapproved opt-in)
  • Phone number not in E.164 format (+1XXXXXXXXXX) — causes silent API failures
  • Stale API key, webhook secret, or auth token
  • Rate limit exceeded — long codes are capped at 1 msg/sec
  • Recipient previously sent STOP (error 21610) — cannot re-message without new opt-in

Fast checks (10–15 minutes)

  • Pull the exact error code from Twilio Console → Monitor → Logs → Messaging
  • Set a StatusCallback URL on your next test send to capture live delivery receipts
  • Reproduce with the smallest possible test case — one number, one message, minimal content
  • Confirm A2P 10DLC campaign status is "Approved" in Twilio Console
  • Check vendor status pages and recent changelogs for carrier policy changes
  • Test with a fresh credential or minimal config to rule out auth issues

What usually fixes it

  • Complete A2P 10DLC brand + campaign registration if not done
  • Strip promotional language, URLs, or trigger words from message body
  • Switch to E.164 format (+1XXXXXXXXXX) for all destination numbers
  • Use Twilio Lookup API to filter landlines and disconnected numbers before send
  • Implement a messaging service pool to manage throughput and avoid throttling
  • Document the fix — carrier rules change quarterly and this will surface again

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Twilio SMS showing "delivered" but the recipient never got it?

A "delivered" status means the carrier accepted the handoff — not that the message hit the handset. Carriers can silently discard messages after acceptance, especially if the sending number fails their A2P registration check or the content matches a spam pattern. Cross-test with numbers on AT&T, Verizon, and T-Mobile to isolate which carrier is dropping it.

What is A2P 10DLC and do I need it?

A2P 10DLC is the US carrier system for registering business SMS numbers. If you send any business messages (notifications, marketing, 2FA) from a standard 10-digit number, you must register your brand and campaign in Twilio Console. Without it, carriers silently filter messages. It's mandatory in 2026.

How do I check Twilio SMS delivery status?

Go to Twilio Console → Monitor → Logs → Messaging for per-message status and error codes. For real-time visibility, add a StatusCallback URL to your API call — Twilio will POST delivery receipts as status changes. Error codes 30003–30008 each map to a specific failure type (see the table above).

Why does Twilio SMS work for some carriers but not others?

Each major carrier (T-Mobile, AT&T, Verizon) runs its own spam filters. A2P registration gaps, content flags, or opt-in compliance failures can block messages on one carrier while others pass. Test with a SIM from each carrier to identify which network is rejecting your messages, then check your campaign registration for that carrier's specific requirements.

What are Twilio SMS rate limits?

Standard 10-digit long codes: 1 message per second. Toll-free numbers: ~3 messages per second. Short codes: up to 100 messages per second. Exceeding the limit causes silent queuing and potential message drops. If you need volume, use a Twilio Messaging Service with a number pool, or upgrade to a short code for high-throughput campaigns.
