Payment Processor Comparison (No BS Edition)

Here's what each option is actually good for, with no sales pitch.

Square

✓ Best for: New businesses, low volume, no monthly fee tolerance.

✗ Watch out: Expensive at high volume (2.6-2.9% + 10-30¢). Limited customization.

Use if: Doing under $5K/month. Switch once you scale.

Stripe

✓ Best for: Online sales, subscriptions, developer-friendly APIs.

✗ Watch out: Not great for retail/in-person. Holds can be aggressive for new accounts.

Use if: You're online-first or need custom checkout flows.

Traditional Merchant Account

✓ Best for: High volume ($10K+/month), lower per-transaction fees.

✗ Watch out: Complex contracts, more paperwork, monthly fees.

Use if: Volume justifies it. Get 2-3 quotes.

PayPal / Venmo

✓ Best for: Peer-to-peer, quick invoicing, existing customer trust.

✗ Watch out: High fees (2.9% + 30¢ or more). Buyer disputes favor customer.

Use if: Casual sales or invoicing. Not ideal for serious retail.

Common Payment Processing Questions

Q: What's a "good" credit card processing rate?

A: Retail (swiped/chip): 1.8-2.3% + 10-15¢. Online/keyed: 2.5-3.0% + 30¢. If you're paying more, you're likely overpaying. Equipment fees should be minimal or zero.

Q: Should I use Stripe or Square?

A: Stripe for online/software businesses. Square for retail/in-person. Both are fine for starting out, but switch to a merchant account once you hit $10K+/month.

Q: What are the hidden fees I should watch for?

A: PCI compliance ($99-200/year), statement fees ($10-20/month), batch fees ($0.10-0.25), early termination ($200-500). Read your contract carefully. Text us your contract — we'll flag traps.

Q: Can I switch processors without downtime?

A: Yes. Most switches take 3-7 days. Run both systems in parallel for 1-2 days to test. We walk you through the process.
