Stablecoin Business FAQ

Real questions about accepting stablecoin payments — answered without crypto marketing nonsense.

What's a stablecoin, actually?

Short version: Digital currency pegged 1:1 to the US dollar. USDC and USDT are the main ones businesses use.

Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum, stablecoins don't fluctuate wildly. $100 USDC = $100 USD, more or less.

Why it matters: You can accept payments digitally without worrying about price swings between when you're paid and when you cash out.

Is it legal for my business?

Yes, if you're operating legally already. Stablecoins are just a payment method, like Venmo or Zelle — but with different rails.

Where it gets tricky:

  • Money transmitter licenses (most payment processors handle this, not you)
  • Know Your Customer (KYC) rules apply just like bank accounts
  • Securities rules don't apply to stablecoins themselves (yet)

If you're selling normal goods/services, you're fine. If you're structuring transactions to avoid reporting, you're not.

What about taxes?

Every stablecoin transaction is a taxable event. The IRS considers it property, not currency.

What that means:

  • You report revenue at the USD value when received
  • If you hold it and it changes value (rare but possible), that's a gain/loss
  • Most businesses convert to USD immediately to avoid complexity

Practical reality: Use a payment processor that auto-converts to USD and provides tax reports. Don't try to DIY this.

Why would I accept stablecoins instead of Stripe?

Lower fees: 0.5–1% vs 2.9% + 30¢. On $10k/month, that's $200–$250 saved.

Instant settlement: Money hits your account in minutes, not days.

No chargebacks: Transactions are final (which is good and bad — see next question).

International payments: No currency conversion fees or delays.

When it doesn't make sense: If your customers don't have wallets, forcing them to set one up adds friction that kills conversions.

What about refunds and disputes?

There are no chargebacks. If a customer wants a refund, you manually send them stablecoins back.

Good: No chargeback fraud. You're protected from scammers claiming "package never arrived" after they received it.

Bad: Legitimate customer disputes require you to act in good faith. There's no Stripe/PayPal mediation.

Real-world fix: Offer hybrid payment options. Stablecoins for customers who want speed/low fees, Stripe for everyone else.

How do I actually get paid?

Option 1: Self-custody wallet (you control your funds, higher risk if you lose access)

Option 2: Business payment processor (like Coinbase Commerce, BitPay, or Solana Pay integrations)

Most businesses choose Option 2 — auto-converts to USD, provides tax reports, handles compliance.

Can I accept it with my existing website/invoicing?

Yes. Most ecommerce platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce) have stablecoin payment plugins.

For invoicing: Stripe started supporting USDC on Polygon. Services like Request Network provide crypto-native invoicing.

Setup time: 30 min to 2 hours depending on your tech stack and comfort level.

What if customers don't have crypto wallets?

Then don't force it. Offer it alongside traditional payment methods.

Reality: Most US consumers don't have wallets yet. Stablecoin payments work best for:

  • International customers avoiding wire fees
  • B2B payments between crypto-native companies
  • High-ticket items where 2.9% saved = real money

What are the risks I'm not being told?

Stablecoin de-pegging: Rare but happened with UST (algorithmic, now dead). USDC and USDT haven't de-pegged significantly, but it's not impossible.

Regulatory changes: Congress could pass laws requiring licenses, reporting, or restrictions. You'd need to adapt.

Technical errors: Send to wrong address = money gone forever. No "undo" button.

Mitigation: Use established processors, convert to USD immediately, don't hold large balances in stablecoins.

Is this the future or just hype?

Honest answer: Stablecoins solve real problems (fees, settlement speed, cross-border friction) but aren't replacing credit cards anytime soon.

They're most valuable for businesses that:

  • Process $50k+/month and 2.9% fees hurt
  • Serve international customers
  • Operate in industries with high chargeback rates

For a local coffee shop or service business with mostly US customers? Probably not worth the learning curve yet.

Bottom Line

Stablecoin payments are legitimate, legal, and can save money — but they're not magic, and crypto evangelists oversell the benefits.

Start small. Test with willing customers. Don't force adoption. Measure actual savings vs setup/maintenance costs.
