Common questions

What is a chargeback?

A chargeback is when a cardholder disputes a charge through their bank instead of contacting you directly. The bank reverses the funds while they investigate — often taking 45–90 days.

How do I win a chargeback dispute?

Submit: proof of delivery, signed order confirmation, communication history, and your refund policy. Respond within the processor's deadline (usually 7–20 days). Clear documentation wins 60–70% of disputes.

How many chargebacks can I get before losing my merchant account?

Most processors flag accounts at 1% chargeback ratio. Above 2% risks account termination by card networks. Monitor monthly.
