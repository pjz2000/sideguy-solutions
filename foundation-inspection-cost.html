Foundation Inspection Cost in San Diego

What a real inspection covers, what it costs, and the one question you must ask before signing anything.

Typical cost range

  • Visual contractor inspection: $300–$600
  • Structural engineer: $500–$1,500
  • Home inspector add-on: $100–$250 extra
  • Free inspections from repair companies: be cautious

What they check

  • Crack patterns and width
  • Settlement or unlevel floors
  • Moisture in crawl space or slab
  • Drainage and grading around house
  • Bolt connections (pre-1980 homes)

San Diego soil reality

  • Expansive clay soils in east SD suburbs
  • Sandy soils near coast: generally stable
  • Hillside homes: check for lateral movement
  • Dry season = shrink, wet season = swell
Clarity before cost: Most cracks in San Diego foundations are cosmetic. Don't let anyone rush you into expensive repairs before an independent engineer has looked at it.

Crack Types: What to Actually Worry About

Not all cracks are equal. A quick guide:

  • Hairline vertical cracks: Very common in poured concrete. Usually cosmetic from normal curing. Monitor over 6 months — if stable, no action needed.
  • Stair-step cracks (block/brick): Indicate differential settlement. Moderate concern. Get a contractor to look.
  • Diagonal cracks from corners of windows/doors: Often minor settlement. Watch for widening. Structural engineer if wider than 1/4 inch.
  • Horizontal cracks: Serious. Indicate lateral soil pressure pressing inward. Structural engineer review immediately.
  • Wide cracks (>1/4 inch) or actively growing: Get a structural engineer — do not wait.

Questions to Ask Before Hiring Anyone

  • Are you a licensed contractor or a licensed structural engineer?
  • Do you have E&O (errors and omissions) insurance?
  • Can I see before/after documentation from 3 similar jobs?
  • What is your warranty — and what voids it?
  • Will you give me a written report I can share with another party?
  • Is this a minimum repair, or are you recommending the maximum scope?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a foundation inspection cost in San Diego?

A basic visual inspection from a licensed contractor runs $300–$600. A structural engineer inspection costs $500–$1,500. If you're buying a home, a combined home + foundation inspection from an independent inspector is often $400–$700.

What does a foundation inspector actually check?

Crack patterns (type, direction, width), settlement signs like unlevel floors or sticking doors, moisture in the crawl space or around the slab, drainage and grading around the perimeter, and in older homes, the state of anchor bolts and cripple walls.

Should I use a foundation repair company for the inspection?

Use them for an initial look, not as your only opinion. They often do free inspections, but they have a financial incentive to recommend repairs. If their quote is over $3,000, spend $400–$600 on an independent structural engineer. That consultation usually pays for itself.

What are typical foundation repair costs in San Diego?

Minor crack injection: $500–$2,500. Mudjacking or slab lifting: $1,500–$5,000. Steel or helical piers: $1,200–$3,500 per pier. Full foundation replacement is rare and extremely expensive. Most San Diego homes with cracks only need monitoring or minor repair.

When should I get a structural engineer instead of a contractor?

Any time you have horizontal cracks, cracks wider than 1/4 inch, actively growing cracks, floors that are noticeably unlevel, or you're buying a home with known foundation issues. A structural engineer's stamped report gives you defensible documentation.
