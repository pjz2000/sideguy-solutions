Stripe vs Square:
Which Is Actually Cheaper?

Both advertise simple flat-rate pricing. But your real effective rate depends on your volume, card mix, and whether you're in-person or online. Here's the honest math.

Who wins in-person vs online

✓ Stripe wins for:

Online-first businesses. Subscription billing. Developers who need API control. Higher average ticket sizes. Multi-currency or global sales.

✓ Square wins for:

Retail / in-person operations. Free POS software. Restaurants (Square for Restaurants). Simple setup, no dev needed. Dispute protection.

The $10k/month rule: Below $10k/month, the difference is small — choose based on your workflow. Above $10k/month, the gap widens fast. At $30k/month online, Stripe saves ~$1,680/year vs Square's online rate. That's real money that should factor into your decision.

The traps nobody tells you about

  • Square hardware lock-in: The free reader is great until you need a full POS setup. Terminals run $299–$799 and are Square-only.
  • Stripe dispute fees: $15 per dispute, win or lose. Square doesn't charge this on standard disputes. If you have a chargeback-prone business, this adds up fast.
  • Square software pricing: The free plan is genuinely good. But Square for Restaurants or Retail runs $60+/month — that's an extra $720/year that doesn't show up in the "2.6%" headline.
  • Stripe account holds: Stripe has aggressive fraud detection. If your sales volume spikes suddenly (good problem) or you're in a flagged category, funds can be held with no warning.
  • Neither is interchange-plus: Both are flat-rate processors. At $30k+/month, interchange-plus pricing from a traditional processor is typically 0.5–0.8% cheaper than both. That's $1,800–$2,880/year at that volume.

When to leave both of them

Flat-rate processors work well up to about $10k–$15k/month. Above that, you're paying a convenience premium. Interchange-plus pricing — where you pay the actual card network rate plus a fixed markup — is almost always cheaper at higher volumes. Most local payment consultants can set this up without long-term contracts.

Quick decision guide

Choose Stripe if:

You sell primarily online, need API/developer access, run subscriptions, or process a high volume where saving 0.3% on CNP matters.

Choose Square if:

You're primarily in-person, want free POS software, need simple hardware, or want dispute protection included.

Look beyond both if:

You're processing $15k+/month consistently. Interchange-plus from a local or regional processor will almost certainly beat both on effective rate.

What about PayPal?

PayPal charges 3.49% + $0.49 for invoices and 2.29% + $0.09 for in-person. It's worse on fees than both Stripe and Square for most business use cases. Its real problem isn't cost — it's account holds. PayPal was built for consumer peer-to-peer, not primary business processing. Funds can be held 21 days without notice if your sales volume spikes or you're in a flagged category. Use it for one-off payments, not as your primary processor.

Alternatives to Stripe and Square

If you're above $15k/month, the best alternative to both is usually interchange-plus pricing from a traditional merchant account (Helcim, Stax, or a local ISO). Below that, Stripe and Square are genuinely the simplest options. Don't let a salesperson convince you to sign a 3-year contract with a processor you've never heard of just to get a slightly lower rate.
