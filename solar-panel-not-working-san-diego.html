San Diego has great solar conditions, so when panels stop producing it's usually an inverter issue, a tripped breaker, or a monitoring app glitch — not a panel failure. You can diagnose 80% of issues yourself before calling anyone.

← Electrical Problems Hub — more San Diego guidance

Check these first (no tools needed)

  • Look at your inverter — is it showing a green light?
  • Red or flashing light = fault code, check the display
  • Check your solar app/monitoring dashboard
  • Look for any tripped breakers in your main panel
  • Check if any tree growth is newly shading panels
  • Power outage? Most inverters shut off automatically (safety)

Common issues and fixes

  • Inverter fault/error: often clears with restart
  • Grid outage: microinverters/hybrids auto-reconnect
  • Dirty panels: cleaning can restore 5–25% output
  • Single panel underperforming: likely a micro-inverter issue
  • Monitoring offline: check Wi-Fi/router, not the panels

When to call a pro

  • Inverter won't restart after 24 hours
  • Multiple error codes / display is blank
  • Physical damage visible on panels or wiring
  • System is more than 5 years old with no maintenance
  • Production is consistently 30%+ below estimate
