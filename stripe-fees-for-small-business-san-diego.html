Quick Answer

Stripe charges 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card transaction. On a $100 sale that's $3.20 (3.2% effective). On a $30 sale it's $1.17 (3.9% effective). On a $10 sale it's $0.59 (5.9% effective). The smaller your average ticket, the harder that fixed 30¢ hits.

Additional charges most people miss: international cards (+1.5%), currency conversion (+1%), disputes ($15 each win or lose until reversed), and Stripe Billing add-ons (0.5–0.8%) if you run subscriptions.

Common Questions

What is Stripe's effective rate for small business?

Stripe's advertised 2.9% + $0.30 becomes your effective rate only at certain ticket sizes. At $100/ticket: ~3.2%. At $50: ~3.5%. At $20: ~4.4%. Most small businesses end up between 3.2% and 3.8% once you factor in card mix, some international transactions, and occasional dispute fees.

Does Stripe charge monthly fees?

No monthly fee on the standard plan — you only pay per transaction. However, add-ons cost extra: Stripe Billing adds 0.5–0.8% for subscription management; Radar for Fraud Teams adds $0.02/txn. Most small businesses on the base plan only pay the per-transaction charge.

When does it make sense to leave Stripe?

When you're processing $10,000+/month consistently and your effective rate is above 3.4%. Or if you primarily take in-person payments where Square or a dedicated terminal solution prices better. Stripe typically negotiates volume pricing at $80K+ per year — below that, you're on flat rate.

What is interchange-plus pricing?

Interchange-plus passes through Visa/Mastercard's actual interchange rate and adds a fixed transparent markup (typically 0.10–0.30% + a small per-transaction fee). Unlike Stripe's bundled flat rate, interchange-plus is transparent — and almost always cheaper above $8k–$10k/month in volume for businesses with a healthy card mix.

Are there Stripe alternatives in San Diego?

Yes. Square (better for in-person, simple setup), interchange-plus merchant accounts through local ISOs, PayPal/Braintree for existing PayPal customer bases, and Helcim or Stax for subscription-model pricing. What's Stripe's actual fee per transaction?

2.9% + $0.30 for US cards online. In-person: 2.7% + $0.05. Manually keyed: 3.4% + $0.30. ACH: 0.8% capped at $5. Add 1.5% for international cards and 1% for currency conversion.

How do I reduce my Stripe fees?

Process more in-person (lower rates). Push B2B clients to ACH (0.8% vs 2.9%). Negotiate volume pricing if you process $80K+/year. Or switch to interchange-plus through a different processor — use the calculator above to see if it's worth it at your volume.
