AI FAQ (extended) — deeper answers for practitioners

Long-form FAQ and practical guidance covering common concerns from data quality to governance, tooling, and ROI for small teams.

Data quality & preparation

Good pilots depend on representative, clean samples. Expect a short data-prep phase: sampling, labeling, and building checks to filter noisy records.

Governance questions

Keep governance lightweight: name an owner, define a review cadence, and log decisions. Escalate to legal for regulated data.

Tooling & vendor selection

Compare tools by privacy, cost, and ease. Ask vendors for sample outputs and failure mode descriptions before committing.

Measuring ROI

Define simple metrics early (time saved, accuracy, revenue impact) and measure them at the pilot checkpoints to calculate payback.

FAQ — extended

How much data is enough?
It depends—some pilots need only hundreds of representative samples, others benefit from thousands; start with a small sample and iterate.
What are good acceptance criteria?
Set clear thresholds for accuracy or percent automated plus a measurement window to assess reliability.
How do we keep audits and reviews simple?
Collect a lightweight evidence pack: sample inputs/outputs, logs, and a short risk assessment for reviewers.
