What actually matters

The biggest factors are power availability (MW-scale), redundancy architecture (N+1, 2N), cooling infrastructure, and execution quality. This is not something you want to learn by trial and error — the stakes are too high and the costs compound fast.

Common decision points

  • Power capacity planning (how much, from where, what backup)
  • Electrical infrastructure design (distribution, redundancy, monitoring)
  • Contractor vetting (who can actually execute at this scale)
  • Timeline and phasing (what can be done in parallel)
  • Cost estimation (avoiding massive overruns)
