AI adoption cost breakdown — how to budget for pilots and rollouts

A practical breakdown of likely costs and how to plan a budget for pilots, fixed work, and ongoing operations.

Pilot costs

Typical pilot costs: $2,500–$8,000 depending on scope, integrations, and data prep. That includes discovery, a small build, testing, and a short measurement window.

Data preparation

Data cleaning, sampling, and minimal labeling are often the largest hidden costs. Estimate a few days to a few weeks for non-trivial data prep depending on quality.

Hosting & API fees

Ongoing costs include cloud hosting, API usage for models, and storage—these vary by usage. Include conservative monthly estimates in quotes.

Ongoing maintenance

Plan for monitoring, retraining, and operational support. A small retainer or hourly support contract is common after a pilot.

FAQ

Why is data prep expensive?
Poor quality data requires more cleaning and sampling, which takes time and human effort.
