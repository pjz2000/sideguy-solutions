What's actually being built

The "future AI economy" isn't a single thing — it's several infrastructure layers being built at the same time, mostly by different teams for different reasons. Here's what's real as of early 2026:

  • AI agents with payment authority. OpenAI's Operator, Anthropic's Claude Agents, and several enterprise platforms now support AI completing purchases within human-defined limits. The infrastructure for agents to hold funds and settle autonomously (rather than just spending a human's card) is being developed in parallel — primarily on Solana and Ethereum infrastructure.
  • Machine-to-machine service procurement. Property management software, logistics platforms, and enterprise procurement systems are automating vendor selection and service ordering for routine tasks. A human sets the parameters; the system executes.
  • Residential energy market integration. Virtual power plants aggregating home batteries are live in California, Texas, and several other states. Homeowners are receiving automated payments for grid services. The next layer — homes directly participating in wholesale energy markets — is in regulatory development.
  • Pay-per-API economies. AI tools increasingly charge per query, per task, or per successful outcome rather than per seat. This changes how business software is priced and makes economic unit calculation more complex.

Timeline: what arrives when

  • Now (2025–2026) AI assistants completing purchases with stored credentials. VPPs paying homeowners for battery dispatch. Automated vendor booking in property management and logistics. API-based service marketplaces for software tasks.
  • Near-term (2026–2028) AI agents with independent payment wallets (stablecoin-based). More widespread residential energy trading programs. Agent-to-agent service procurement for routine physical tasks. Broader business directory access optimized for machine consumption.
  • Medium-term (2028–2032) Retail-level direct participation in wholesale energy markets. Autonomous business entities (DAOs, AI-run service providers) operating as real market participants. Peer-to-peer energy trading between homes.
  • Long-term (2032+) Most routine business-to-business transactions initiated and settled without human involvement. AI agents as primary customers for many service categories. Fully autonomous energy nodes in homes and microgrids.

What operators actually need to do right now

Most of this is infrastructure-layer change, not something that requires immediate action from every business. The one area that's immediately relevant: making your business machine-readable. That means structured data on your website, consistent business information across directories, online booking, and the ability to accept card payment digitally. These are good practices anyway — and they also position you for whatever the next procurement layer looks like.
