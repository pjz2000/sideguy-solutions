Moving to San Diego: What Nobody Tells You

The brochure version of San Diego is gorgeous. The real version is also gorgeous — but your SDG&E bill will be $400, your HVAC quote will be $12,000, and the contractor you hired from Yelp won't call back. This guide is the honest version.

North County Neighborhood Reality Check

North County isn't one market — Encinitas, Carlsbad, and Solana Beach each have distinct home ages, HOA structures, and contractor dynamics. Here's what to expect.

Encinitas

Surf culture, good schools, and mostly older homes. That older stock means older HVAC systems, cast iron or galvanized plumbing, and deferred maintenance that surfaces in year one. Budget $3,000–$8,000 for your first year's surprises.

Carlsbad

Newer construction, higher HOA fees, and a strong contractor network — but premium pricing throughout. Before you call anyone for a repair, check what your HOA actually covers. You may be paying for a service you can get free.

Solana Beach

Tight inventory and premium price per square foot. Ocean proximity means salt air corrosion on HVAC condensers every 8–12 years — plan for it. Contractors in this zip code price accordingly.

Who To Call When Things Break

Every new homeowner in North County hits at least one of these in year one. Here's the quick guide before you panic.

HVAC

Always get 3 quotes — variance of 40–60% on the same job is normal in this market. Text PJ before you sign anything over $2,000. Compressor replacements that "require full system replacement" often don't.

Plumbing

Repiping quotes in San Diego range from $4,000 to $18,000 for identical jobs. Get itemized line items. Any quote that bundles "labor + materials" without breaking out footage is hiding something.

Electrical

Panel upgrades for EV chargers are the most common first-year electrical job. A permit is required — if your contractor says they'll skip it, that's your exit signal. You can pull the permit yourself if needed.

Solar

NEM 3.0 changed the payback math significantly. Without battery storage, payback periods can double. Any solar pitch that doesn't address NEM 3.0 specifically is working from 2022 numbers.

Red Flags From Real North County Experiences

  • Contractor calls back within an hour, pushes for same-day signing, and "has a slot opening up tomorrow." Urgency is a sales tactic, not a scheduling reality.
  • Quote arrives as a single lump sum with no line items. You have no way to compare it — which is the point.
  • HVAC tech diagnoses a refrigerant issue but won't tell you what refrigerant type your system uses. Some older R-22 systems require full replacement — but the tech should say so, not imply it.
  • Roofer estimates "a few squares" of damage after a storm without going on the roof. If they didn't climb up, they didn't inspect it.
  • Plumber says the pipe "has to be replaced" but can't show you where or why on inspection footage. Camera inspection is standard — if they don't have one, find someone who does.
  • Any contractor who requires full cash payment upfront on a job over $500. California law limits deposits to 10% or $1,000, whichever is less, for licensed contractors.
