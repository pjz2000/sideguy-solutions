AI pricing & packages — clear ranges and example scenarios

We provide plain-language price ranges, sample scenarios, and guidance on how to budget for pilots and small rollouts.

Typical ranges

  • Pilot (2–4 weeks): $2,500–$8,000
  • Fixed-scope project: starting at $8,000
  • Ongoing support: monthly retainers or pay-as-you-go

How we price

Pricing depends on the scope, data preparation, and integration complexity. Pilots are quoted with clear deliverables and success metrics so there are no surprises.

What’s included

  • Discovery & scoping
  • Pilot build and test
  • Runbooks, training, and handoff

Payment terms & contracts

Typical pilot terms: 50% upfront and 50% on delivery of the agreed MVP. Retainers are billed monthly with a simple cancellation policy and a short notice period.

Example scenarios & budgets

  • Auto-response pilot — $3,500 (includes discovery, initial set-up, and a 2-week measurement window)
  • Invoice automation pilot — $4,500 (includes integration with a payments workflow and a 3-week measurement window)
  • Inventory forecasting pilot — $6,000 (requires sample historical data and a short validation period)

Ongoing costs to plan for

Beyond project fees, expect variable monthly costs: hosting, API usage, and data storage. We estimate these and include them in the project quote where possible.

Pricing FAQ

What does a pilot cost?
Pilots typically range $2,500–$8,000 depending on scope and data needs.
Do you offer retainers?
Yes—monthly retainers for ongoing support are available, or pay-as-you-go arrangements for ad-hoc work.
Are there ongoing hosting or API fees?
Yes—these are variable and depend on usage; we provide estimates and options to minimize ongoing fees.
How do you estimate ROI?
We compare pilot costs to time savings, accuracy improvements, and any direct revenue impact to calculate simple payback periods and expected ROI.
