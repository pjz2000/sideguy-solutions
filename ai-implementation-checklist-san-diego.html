AI implementation checklist — plan, run, and measure a pilot

A concise, actionable checklist for small teams to plan a low-risk pilot: discovery, data prep, build, measurement, and handoff.

SideGuy: This checklist is a practical guide, not a guarantee—adapt each step to your environment and compliance needs.

Overview

Use the checklist to keep pilots focused and measurable. The checklist assumes a 2–6 week pilot with a small scope and clear success metrics.

Pre-pilot: discover & decide

  1. Identify a clear use case with measurable outcomes
  2. List stakeholders, owners, and success criteria
  3. Confirm accessible sample data for the pilot
  4. Estimate budget and timeline (2–6 weeks)

Data prep & privacy

  • Sample, clean, and label data as needed
  • Remove or anonymize sensitive fields for pilots
  • Document data flows and retention policies
  • Define access controls and logging

Build & test

  • Start small: build an MVP to prove the metric
  • Include fallbacks and human-in-the-loop checks
  • Test with real inputs and monitor failure modes

Measure & decide

Collect metrics daily or weekly; compare with baseline and meet the predefined decision point. Decide to iterate, scale, or stop based on results.

Handoff & operations

  • Create runbooks and monitoring dashboards
  • Schedule training and set an owner for the workflow
  • Plan a phased rollout with rollback criteria

Quick checklist (printable)

  • Use case
  • Success metrics (1–3)
  • Sample data confirms
  • Timeline & budget
  • MVP built and tested
  • Metrics collected and decision made

FAQ

How long should a pilot be?
2–6 weeks is typical for a focused pilot.
Who should own the pilot?
A single product or operations owner who reports progress and decisions.
