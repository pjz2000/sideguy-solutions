Quick Checks You Can Do Right Now

Before you call anyone, spend 2–3 minutes doing the basics. For most problems, 20–40% of issues disappear once you check the obvious-but-easy-to-miss things.

  • Notice when this started — after weather, guests, updates, new tools, or renovations.
  • Write down any warning lights, error messages, smells, sounds, or leaks.
  • Try safe resets only — breakers, routers, apps, logins, or valves (no risky improvising).
  • Take 2–3 photos and, if it’s visual, a 10–15 second video from your phone.
Urgency Check
Stop & Text PJ Immediately If You See:
  • Water where it clearly shouldn’t be or active leaking.
  • Burning smell, smoke, hot outlets, or sparking panels.
  • Gas smell or “rotten egg” odor anywhere inside.
  • Security, money, or data issues that feel like they can’t wait.

If any of these are happening, don’t overthink it — text PJ and say what you’re seeing.

How SideGuy Thinks About Problems

Whether it’s a payment issue, a leak, a broken system, or a software glitch, we use the same pattern:

  • Get the facts: what changed, what’s broken, what’s the risk window.
  • Sort the problem into: home, tech, money, or operations.
  • Run a short decision tree: safe DIY vs. call a pro vs. “text PJ and triage it.”
  • Leave you with a clear next step and, if needed, a follow-up plan.
