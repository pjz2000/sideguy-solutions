AI case studies — short, practical examples

Real examples of small teams running focused pilots and the outcomes they saw—time saved, accuracy gains, and practical lessons learned.

Auto-response pilot (customer messages)

Problem: High volume of repetitive inquiries causing slow response times. Pilot: small model plus rules to auto-respond to simple inquiries and escalate complex ones. Outcome: triage time reduced by ~70%, staff could handle more exceptions. Cost: $3,500 pilot; payback within 6 weeks.

Invoice routing and automation

Problem: Manual routing of invoices across departments. Pilot: use an AI model to extract vendor, amount, and match rules into the payment queue. Outcome: manual routing reduced by 60% and fewer missed payments.

Simple inventory forecasting

Problem: Stockouts on high-velocity items. Pilot: model trained on 6 months of sales data to suggest reorder quantities. Outcome: forecasting accuracy improved, and stockouts fell by 20% in the pilot window.

Key lessons

  • Start small with a measurable outcome
  • Keep humans in the loop and define fallbacks
  • Measure against a clear baseline

FAQ

Do pilots generalize?
Some do—pilots help you learn what needs scale and what needs additional data or governance.
