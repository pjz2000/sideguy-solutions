AI FAQ

Short answers to the questions people usually ask when they start thinking about AI for their team.

Who this FAQ is for

Operators and small teams considering AI pilots who want plain-language answers about risks, costs, and what a pilot looks like.

Common use cases

  • Auto-responding to customer messages
  • Invoice automation and routing
  • Basic forecasting and reorder suggestions

Getting started

We recommend a scoped pilot, a clear success metric, and data access that can be audited; a small pilot is the fastest way to learn.

Governance & privacy

Document data flows, limit access, and define retention policies. If you handle regulated data, we add extra controls and legal checks.

Measuring success

Pick 1–3 metrics (time saved, percent auto-handled, error rate) and decide in advance when the pilot is a success.

FAQ

How long does an AI pilot take?
Typically 2–6 weeks depending on scope and data readiness.
Do we need an internal ML engineer?
Not for pilots — we run practical experiments and hand off clear operational steps for your team.
What about privacy and compliance?
We design governance and data-handling rules that match your risk profile, and can run pilots without live sensitive data if needed.
What happens after a successful pilot?
We document the build, prepare runbooks, and outline costs and timelines for a phased rollout.
How do you measure ROI?
Define metrics up front and measure actual time saved, accuracy improvements, or revenue impact at pilot checkpoints.
