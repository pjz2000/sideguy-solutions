🚰 Most Common Plumbing Issues (San Diego-Specific)

1. Low Water Pressure

  • Why it happens here: Hard water buildup in pipes (San Diego water is very hard)
  • Check first: Aerator on faucet (unscrew, clean, reinstall — fixes 40% of cases)
  • Cost if plumber needed: $150-400 (pressure regulator replacement)

2. Running Toilet

  • Why it happens: Flapper valve wears out (especially in older homes)
  • DIY fix: $15 flapper kit from Home Depot, 20 min install (YouTube it)
  • Plumber cost if DIY fails: $120-200
  • Hidden cost: Running toilet wastes 200+ gallons/day = $100+/month on water bill

3. Clogged Drains

  • Kitchen sink: Grease buildup (San Diego's hard water makes it worse)
  • Bathroom sink: Hair + soap scum
  • Shower: Hair (always hair)
  • DIY first: Remove P-trap, clean manually (drain cleaners damage pipes)
  • Plumber cost: $150-300 for snake/auger

4. Water Heater Problems

  • No hot water: Pilot light out (gas) or breaker tripped (electric) — check these first
  • Not enough hot water: Sediment buildup (flush tank annually)
  • Leaking: Call plumber immediately (tank failure = flood risk)
  • Repair cost: $200-500 / Replacement: $1,200-2,500

5. Slab Leaks

  • Why it matters in SD: Many homes built on concrete slabs, pipes underneath
  • Signs: Hot spots on floor, water bill spike, foundation cracks
  • Cost: $2,000-$10,000 (depends on access, rerouting vs repair)
  • Urgent: Yes — can damage foundation

🏠 San Diego-Specific Plumbing Challenges

Hard Water: San Diego has some of the hardest water in California (300-400 ppm). This causes:

  • Mineral buildup in pipes & faucets
  • Water heater sediment (reduces lifespan)
  • Soap scum in drains
  • Solution: Water softener ($800-$2,500 installed) or descaling service ($150-300/year)

Older Homes (Pre-1970): May have galvanized pipes (rusts from inside). If you're in North Park, Normal Heights, City Heights — check this.

Drought Restrictions: Some older low-flow fixtures cause drainage issues. Upgrading to modern low-flow = better performance.

Tree Roots: Eucalyptus and palm roots seek water. If you have old clay sewer lines + mature trees = root intrusion risk.

🛠️ DIY vs Call a Plumber

Safe to DIY:

  • Replacing faucet aerators
  • Installing new showerhead
  • Cleaning P-trap under sink
  • Toilet flapper replacement
  • Tightening visible pipe connections

Call a plumber for:

  • Anything involving gas lines
  • Main water line issues
  • Sewer line problems
  • Water heater replacement
  • Anything requiring a permit
  • If you're unsure (water damage > plumber cost)

🚨 How to Not Get Ripped Off

Red flags:

  • "We need to replace everything" without clear explanation
  • Cash-only, no receipt offered
  • No license number on truck/website (CA requires C-36 license for plumbers)
  • Pressure to decide immediately
  • Quote without seeing the problem in person

Good signs:

  • Licensed C-36 contractor in California
  • Explains the problem and options (not just "this is what you need")
  • Written estimate before work starts
  • Warranty on work (1-2 years typical)
  • Reviews mentioning trustworthiness (not just "fast service")
